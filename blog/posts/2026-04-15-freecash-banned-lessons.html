🍎 The #2 App on the US App Store Just Got Banned by Apple

By Malcolm Lowry X Bluesky · March 19, 2026 · 5 min read

Table of contents

    FreeCash, a reward app with 6 million downloads per month, was pulled Monday for fraudulent practices. Fake reviews, deceptive ads, illegal data harvesting.

    Moritz, 22 year old CEO of Almedia, the company who makes FreeCash, posted on Linkedin talking about how FreeCash was removed from the AppStore. I even posted a supportive reply and tried to help. But then I read the Tech Crunch article. My jaw dropped. I deleted my post.

    As someone who's spent 17 years in mobile gaming user acquisition and online advertising, I have seen most of these tactics employed before, but never all at once so brazenly.

    We call these things "Blackhat" in the online advertising and acquisition world. For those who are not in this space I will give a quick breakdown of what happened:

    What Happened: The Blackhat Playbook

    First, it looks to me that all of this arose from some obsession to reach top downloaded in the Google Play and Apple App Store. I have seen this before, it usually comes from CEOs either for strategic reasons (to harvest free organic installs from people viewing the top downloaded apps list), hype or investor baiting, or ego.

    To reach the top globally downloaded apps you need millions of installs, at least that's what we can assume. The actual mechanics of how these lists work is proprietary.

    So how did FreeCash hit the top of those charts? It CAN be done naturally, apps like WhatsApp, ChatGpt, Claude, Google Maps, all earn those spots legitimately. But FreeCash didn't do it naturally. It employed basically every blackhat tactic under the sun.

    🚩 Blackhat Tactics Used

    • Illegal user data harvesting - It's one thing to ask users about themselves, it's another thing entirely to collect that information without them knowing.
    • Bot traffic - Tricking the appstores into thinking your app is getting more traffic to its app page and installs than it really is.
    • Misleading or Bait and Switch ads - Some FreeCash TikTok ads were claiming users can earn money just by scrolling TikTok (which is, in fact a lie).

    This all amounted to hitting up to 6 million installs at the peak in February with estimates from the TechCrunch article at 3 million for April. That drop is what happens when you turn off the bots.

    So Who Cares?

    This isn't the first time. In 2020, Mintegral, a major ad SDK used by thousands of apps, got caught doing similar things. They were collecting click data to hijack attribution, using JavaScript backdoors to hide malicious behavior, and obfuscating their code to avoid detection.

    FreeCash just got caught doing the same playbook, but bigger.

    FreeCash was making hundreds of millions of dollars from this while collecting sensitive data (race, religion, sexual orientation, health) from millions of users. And we still don't know what they did with all that data. It's entirely possible they sold it to data brokers. We can't prove it, but the risk is real.

    Why This Matters for the Industry

    It also matters because the rewarded industry has been booming, largely thanks to FreeCash building awareness. The business model itself is totally legitimate. It's not a Ponzi scheme, not a pyramid scheme, not a scheme at all. It's a legitimate industry solving a real problem: connecting advertisers with engaged users.

    But stunts like this hurt the entire category. Legitimate developers suffer. Users lose trust.

    Why I'm Still Building

    The weird thing is, I am just coming out of stealth this week on my own rewarded app: FarmCash. And I'm entering a category that just imploded.

    But that's exactly why it needs to be rebuilt right.

    I never planned to push it to the limit. I've seen what happens when you do. I know the pitfalls.

    I'm building FarmCash on a different foundation: fraud prevention from day one, not as an afterthought. Keeping traffic clean is better for everyone, advertisers, players and partners, even if it means you make a little less.

    ✅ How We're Building FarmCash Differently

    • Transparent economics - You know where the money comes from (game developers pay us, we share it with you)
    • Fraud prevention first - Built into the core, not bolted on later
    • No data harvesting - We collect what's needed for payouts, nothing more
    • Open about our stack - I'll publish our fraud prevention approach once we launch (no black boxes)

    The Path Forward

    The industry needs this wake-up call. Users deserve better. Rewarded apps aren't going anywhere. But this is a major wakeup call.

    As rewarded app developers we are providing a service to both advertisers and our userbase. And we rely on strong partnerships. If we try to swindle or deceive any part of that mix, we will fail.

    Users and advertisers deserve better. Let's build it. In public. The right way.
